Testimonies:

e Richard Sierra. Davidson Center, volunteer. Recommends funding for youth
programming.

e Thomasina Bushby. Community Board 5 member. Recommends funding for anti-
violence for youth and remedial education services.

e Frank Rojas. Perseverance Project. The Project gathers oral histories of lived
experiences. These stories are then transformed into performances. This project is being
done in the hopes of starting a movement of local empowerment. They would like to be
involved in local schools.

e Carlos Rosario. Community Board 5 member and Health and Human Services
Committee co-chair. There are many children in the district, but a dearth of services for
them. There needs to be innovative and mindful programs. There needs to be a shift in
our community into a ‘Wellness Community.’

e Shenia Rudolph. Community Board 5 member. There needs to be more programs like
the Police Athletic League. There are many incidents of youth violence against the
elderly. As there is more development, schools will become more overcrowded. There
needs to be more money for the Police Athletic League, the Davidson Community Center
and the Police Explorers. There needs to be more programs for youth to be more
involved with the community and for there to be ‘Safe Havens’ in the community.

o Inspector Scheublin has been participating in many local events over the
past few months, including an announcement of the “Every Block Counts”
program at Bean Morris Garden. Mayor Adams was in attendance. Insp.
Scheublin thanks Carrie and Marlene of Bean Morris Garden.

" Overview of program: Focuses on residential blocks within two NYPD
precincts, including the 46", known for having the highest number of
violent crimes, in particular shootings, over the past five years.

can do as a team to let the city know that the city cares. The blocks
identified in the area have had no shootings in 56 days. There were 3
shootings in the same period last year.

e Dr. Tonie McKenzie, MD

o Works at Walton Family Center

o Came to speak about INT 299-2024, which targets food waste. It would
require the Department of Sanitation and the Department of Consumer
Affairs to establish a food donation program where food service
establishments could have the civil penalties for eligible violations waived if

they agree to donate their excess food.

o Dr. McKenzie shared a story of a family that came in for yearly check-in and
teens expressed that they had a limited diet. Approximately 1.3 million report
food insecurity.

" Under INT 299, food businesses can have citations waved and
mitigate fines if they participate in food donation programs. The bill
has been stalled for a year.

01 / The Cut

Robert Moses cut a 6.3-mile trench through the South Bronx

Between 1948 and 1972, the Cross Bronx Expressway displaced over 60,000 residents. Seventy years later, the trench remains an active instrument of environmental harm -- trapping diesel particulate matter, amplifying noise, and concentrating respiratory illness in communities already defined by disinvestment.

04 / Depth Estimation

Measuring enclosure at street level

Monocular depth estimation maps reveal the canyon geometry invisible from above. The trench walls close in -- buildings, barriers, and overpasses compress the vertical field of view to as little as 16% sky.

45

Canyon aspect ratio: 0.64

At the Jerome-Webster section, a person standing inside the trench sees 84% less sky than someone one block north. The geometry of the expressway measurably restricts the sky available to the people living beside it.
